About display: blog

display: blog was created, and is maintained by me, B. Patrick Cox, Jr., a Durham, North Carolina-based front-end web developer. I decided to create this blog because I have historically found that I learn best by teaching. There's an old saying that if you can't explain something to a five-year-old, you don't really understand it, and I believe that to be true. You can expect tutorials here to be written and explained more in layman's terms as opposed to the technical jargon you'll find in most documentation. I was, and often times still feel like, an outsider in the world of web development. Even the most seasoned developers still feel like imposters sometimes.

My goal here is to make front-end development as approachable as possible. However, please keep in mind most of the tutorials here are not geared for the complete novice. They assume you know at least a little bit about HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. If you don't, don't give up yet. I highly recommend you head over to Codecademy and start there, beginning with their easy to follow introductory courses to HTML and CSS, and then proceed to JavaScript.

How I Arrived Here

My path to arriving at a career in web development was as unconvential as one could possible imagine. I don't have a computer science degree, but I do have a college degree...in business administration. Before becoming a front-end developer I bounced around in all sorts of industries - from a car rental agency, to business loan officer, to customer service, to merchandising, to retail. After an early onset mid-life crisis in 2015, I decided it was time to make a major change in my life, and decided to try my hand at web development, something I'd be genuinely curious about for many years. I learned enough over the course of six months to be dangerous via Thinkful, with the assistance of a great mentor, J.P. DeVries, secured my first job in the summer of 2016, and the rest is history.
